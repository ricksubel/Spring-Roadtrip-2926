🚦 Traffic, Rush Hour & Construction Alerts

  • Atlanta (I-75 / I-285 interchange): One of the worst interchanges in the Southeast. On Sunday May 10 — leave Decatur before 10am or after 12pm to avoid the weekend shopping and event traffic that backs up the I-285 connector. I-285 itself can be slow any time of day near I-85 and I-75.
  • I-66 E into DC (Tue May 12 evening): This corridor has HOV-3 requirements and tolls during peak hours (roughly 5:30–9:30am and 3–7pm). Leaving at 5:30pm you'll catch the tail end of outbound evening rush. By 7–8pm it's clear. The toll can be steep during peak ($15–50+) but drops to free/near-free off-peak. You'll be entering around 9–10pm — no toll concerns at that hour.
  • I-95 Baltimore corridor (Fri May 15 evening): The section from the DC Beltway through Baltimore and into Delaware is consistently one of the worst stretches in the country on Friday afternoons. The bottleneck at the I-695 Baltimore Beltway and the Fort McHenry Tunnel clears by around 8pm. Waiting until 6:30–7pm before departing Cherry Hill Park is strongly recommended.
  • Delaware Memorial Bridge: On your Friday May 15 route (I-295 N). Eastbound toll ~$6. Can have backups on Friday evenings heading toward NJ — usually clears after 7pm.
  • NJ / I-78 overpass clearances: Stay on I-78 E and I-80 E through New Jersey with the camper. Local GPS shortcuts through residential NJ towns often involve overpasses and low bridges. Ignore any GPS routing that takes you off the interstate.
  • NYS Thruway (I-90) tolls: The Thruway runs from the NY-PA border through Buffalo and into central NY. Cash and E-ZPass accepted. Expect ~$8–15 westbound from Finger Lakes to Niagara. Keep change or card accessible at toll plazas.
  • PA I-90 construction (Erie area): PennDOT has had ongoing corridor work on I-90 through the Erie metro. Check PENNDOT.gov or 511PA.com a week before your May 29 departure. Night driving (after 5pm) typically encounters fewer active work zones.
  • Memorial Day weekend traffic (May 24–25): Heavy northbound traffic on I-81 N and I-86 W heading into the Finger Lakes and Adirondacks on Memorial Day weekend. Departing Sunday morning (May 24) ahead of the main wave is the right call. Expect campground access roads and state park parking to be very busy Mon May 25.
  • Niagara Falls State Park parking (May 27–28): Parking fills fast in season. Lots #2 and #3 on Goat Island are good alternatives if Lot #1 at Prospect Street is full. Consider arriving early in the morning (before 9am) if you want a prime spot.
  • Apps to use: Waze or Google Maps for live traffic re-routing · GasBuddy for fuel prices along the route · 511 (state DOT apps — 511VA, 511NY, 511PA, 511OH) for construction and incident alerts · iOverlander for RV-specific routing concerns.

🚛 Big-Rig & Towing Notes

  • NY-14 along Seneca Lake: Gorgeous two-lane with narrow shoulders in places. Perfectly manageable at 45–50 mph with a 27-ft trailer — just don't rush. Wineries and pullouts are frequent.
  • Niagara Falls downtown: Drive the Silverado to the park. Downtown Niagara Falls streets were not designed for long trailers — tourist buses and narrow lanes make it genuinely unpleasant. HTR Niagara on Grand Island is only 15 min from the falls.
  • Fuel planning: GasBuddy for current prices. I-81 and I-90 Ohio have frequent Flying J, Pilot, and Love's truck stops with easy pull-through lanes and diesel. Tank up before heading into downtown areas anywhere on this trip.
  • Level & setup priority: Budget 30–45 min for setup at each new campsite. Call ahead on any night you're arriving after 9pm to confirm late check-in procedures — Cherry Hill Park and HTR Niagara both have 24-hr check-in.
